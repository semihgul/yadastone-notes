# Evidence and Trial

## Design goal

Evidence creates suspicion—not automatic truth. Investigation and persuasive argument must be fun while allowing lies, framing, and mistaken conclusions.

## Evidence types

- witness testimony
- blood, footprints, or a found weapon
- servant or guard report
- poison-purchase record
- alibi conflict
- intercepted rumor or correspondence

| Quality | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Weak | suggestive; easy to challenge |
| Corroborated | supported by an independent source |
| Strong | connects a player, act, and time directly |
| Compromised | forged, bribed, incomplete, or biased |

## Trial flow

1. An accusation is brought to council.
2. Players present evidence, explanations, witnesses, or counter-claims.
3. Players and factions negotiate publicly and privately.
4. The council votes: release, imprisonment, exile, or execution.
5. The result affects relationships, reputation, and faction support.

## Safeguards

- Wrong punishment is always possible.
- Trial is not a one-button elimination path.
- The accused retains counterplay through alibis, witnesses, favors, and political support.

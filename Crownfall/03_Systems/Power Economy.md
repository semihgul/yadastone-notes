# Power Economy

## Goal

Crownfall uses competing forms of power. Gaining one should normally cost another resource, create exposure, or impose a future obligation.

| Resource | Role | Typical weakness |
|---|---|---|
| Wealth | buys options, favors, logistics | cannot directly win |
| Public Support | long-term legitimacy and unrest control | changes slowly |
| Noble Support | political votes and elite leverage | transactional and volatile |
| Church Support | legitimacy and moral protection | conflicts with scandal and cruelty |
| Military Strength | protection, coercion, coups | expensive to maintain |
| Information | evidence, secrets, blackmail | must be verified and protected |
| Fear | deterrence and obedience | damages trust; can provoke resistance |
| Reputation | credibility | fragile after hypocrisy or false charges |

> Every action that grants power must cost resources, create risk, reduce another form of power, or impose a future obligation.

| Action | Gain | Cost / exposure |
|---|---|---|
| Raise taxes | wealth | public and noble support decline |
| Host a feast | noble/public goodwill | wealth cost; assassination opportunity |
| Fund the church | church support | wealth cost |
| Execute a suspect | fear, possible safety | reputation loss if evidence is weak |
| Recruit soldiers | military strength | recurring wealth drain |

Players see understandable bands such as “supportive”, “uncertain”, and “hostile”, rather than the complete simulation values.

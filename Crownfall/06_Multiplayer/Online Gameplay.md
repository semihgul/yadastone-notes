# Online Gameplay

## Session identity

Crownfall is a 4–8 player shared-world political contest. The core experience is in-game social interaction, not out-of-game coordination.

## Social rules

- proximity voice is the default conversation tool
- council meetings create a public forum and voting moment
- private spaces enable deals, conspiracies, and deception
- no universal activity feed reveals secret actions

## Alliances

Alliances are player-made agreements, not permanent teams. They can involve promises, exchanged information, mutual protection, or coordinated votes—and may end at any time.

## Anti-grief principles

- No strategy should let a group permanently lock another player out without cost.
- Betrayal is valid gameplay; harassment is not.
- Repeated arbitrary punishment must not become optimal play.
- Reporting, muting, moderation, and accessibility belong to production planning.

## Open decision

The MVP must define the post-death player experience: spectator with information limits, short sessions, or a limited post-death role.

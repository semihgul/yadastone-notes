# Design Pillars

## Multiple paths to victory

Assassination is possible but never the universal best answer. Political legitimacy, public support, church endorsement, noble support, military force, and regency are all intended victory routes.

## Social manipulation is core gameplay

Players must ask: Who benefits? Who is lying? What evidence is reliable? Can this ally be trusted?

## Consequences create stories

Important decisions create visible and delayed outcomes. Raising taxes can solve an immediate problem but create unrest, rumors, and future vulnerability.

## Imperfect information, not opaque randomness

Players receive leads, witnesses, rumors, and social cues—not complete numeric truth. The game preserves agency and counterplay.

## Embodied politics

Meetings, negotiations, investigations, and conspiracies happen in a shared world rather than only through menus.
